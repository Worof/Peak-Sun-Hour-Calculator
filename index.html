Calculator Notes:

This Peak Sun Hours Calculator is designed to estimate the peak sun hours for a specific location and solar panel setup. Here's what the calculator takes into account:

  • The Solar Panel Tilt Angle (degrees from horizontal): This angle determines how tilted the solar panel is relative to the ground. It affects the amount of sunlight the panel can capture.
  • The Solar Panel Azimuth Angle (degrees clockwise from north): This angle specifies the direction the solar panel faces. It influences how the panel aligns with the sun's path during the day.
  • Sunrise Time and Sunset Time: These times indicate the local sunrise and sunset for your location.

Please note that this calculator does not take the following factors into account:

  • Seasonal Variations: The calculator assumes a fixed tilt and azimuth angle and does not consider seasonal changes in the sun's position.
  • Shading and Obstructions: It does not consider shading from nearby objects, buildings, or trees that might affect solar panel efficiency.

Data Source:

This calculator utilizes solar position calculations from the suncalc library (v1.8.0).
